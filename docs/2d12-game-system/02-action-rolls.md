# Action Rolls

## Overview

All uncertain actions, including attacks, skill checks, and certain spell uses, are resolved with an action roll.

## Rolling the Dice

Roll two d12s for every action roll. One must be a distinct Complication die. Compare the final total to the Challenge Threshold set by the GM.

## Outcomes

- Success: total > CT and the Complication die is higher.
- Success with Complication: total > CT and the Complication die is lower.
- Failure: total <= CT and the Complication die is higher.
- Failure with Complication: total <= CT and the Complication die is lower.
- Critical Success: total > CT and both dice match.
- Critical Failure: total <= CT and both dice match.

## Advantage and Disadvantage

Advantage: roll 1 extra d12 and discard the highest result. Disadvantage: roll 1 extra d12 and discard the lowest result.

## Push

Pushing allows a character to reroll a failed check at a cost of 1 Stress (lose 1 Vitality or 1 Resolve depending on whether a physical or mental attribute was used). Fumbles and Critical Failures cannot be pushed.

## Extended Checks

Some tasks are too complex or time-consuming to resolve with a single Check. Use an Extended Check when the GM sets a threshold, frequency, and deadline.
