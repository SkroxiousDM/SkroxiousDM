# Superiority

Superiority is a shared group resource that represents the momentum and advantage your party has built over their enemies. It fluctuates throughout combat as the fortunes of each side rise and fall. Each turn, any character may spend the party\'s Superiority to add a bonus equal to its current value to a single Skill Check of their choice. This bonus may only be applied once per turn, resetting at the start of the next. Superiority also interacts with an NPC statistic called Resolve when the party\'s total Superiority exceeds the Resolve of their enemies---or their leader, if one is present---those enemies become Desperate.[file:3]

## Gaining Superiority Before Combat

Superiority is always gained against a specific enemy or group. Intelligence gathered against a gang of mercenaries, for example, offers no advantage when the party is ambushed by cultists. Regardless of preparation, the maximum Superiority that can be gained before combat is 3. There are three ways to gain Superiority before battle:

### Know Thy Foe
Learning vital and exploitable information about your enemy grants 1 Superiority. It is not sufficient to know that your enemies carry swords. Learning that their helmets restrict their field of vision, that they have no ranged weapons, or that they have never faced a determined opponent---that is the kind of intelligence that changes a fight.[file:3]

### Know Thy Battlefield
Understanding where the battle will be fought can be as valuable as any weapon. Identifying a tactical advantage in the terrain grants 1 Superiority. This might involve scouting an ambush site, seizing the high ground, locating kill zones, finding an unexpected approach, or learning specific Environmental traits of the area.[file:3]

### Know Thy Players
Inspired and unorthodox strategies deserve reward. When a party devises a genuinely clever scheme to gain advantage over their enemies, the GM may award 1 Superiority. The GM should be discerning, but not ungenerous---good ideas should be encouraged.[file:3]

## Gaining and Losing Superiority During Combat

Superiority may increase during combat in the following ways:
- Defeating the enemy leader.
- Defeating an enemy elite.
- Defeating a number of troops in a single turn equal to or greater than their Resolve.[file:3]

Superiority is hard-won and easily lost. The party loses 1 Superiority each time one of the following occurs:
- The first time each party member receives a Critical Wound.
- A party member is defeated.
- The party is caught off-guard by unexpected reinforcements or a sudden shift in the battlefield.[file:3]

## Resolve

Every NPC has a Resolve score representing their willingness to continue fighting. While a default value is provided for each NPC, the GM should treat it as a baseline and adjust it freely to reflect circumstances. When a leader is present on the field, their followers typically use the leader\'s higher Resolve rather than their own. NPCs defending their home territory gain 1 Resolve. NPCs who have little to gain from the fight may have their Resolve reduced by 1. An NPC whose Resolve is reduced to 0 has no interest in a fight and will only enter combat to protect themselves or their honour.[file:3]

When the party\'s Superiority exceeds an NPC\'s Resolve at the start of that NPC\'s turn, they become Desperate---unless the GM rules otherwise.[file:3]

## The Desperate Condition

Desperate is a condition that applies only to NPCs. A Desperate character knows their position is untenable and will look for a way out. This does not guarantee surrender or flight---some enemies have nowhere to run, and others are too fanatical or mindless to back down even when the odds are hopeless. The GM should choose whichever response best fits the situation:
- **Flee**: If the NPCs can withdraw, step aside, or otherwise disengage, they do so.
- **Surrender**: If surrender offers a reasonable chance of survival, they take it.
- **Charge**: With no escape and nothing left to lose, they break cover and throw everything into one final desperate assault.

Once enemies become Desperate, the GM should begin working toward bringing the combat to a close. The outcome is likely decided---unless something changes the situation dramatically.[file:3]

## Social Superiority

Social Conflicts follow the same Superiority framework as combat. There are three ways to build Superiority before a Social Conflict begins.
### Know Thy Foe
Vital or secret information about the target grants 1 Superiority per piece of intelligence. Unlike other forms of preparation, Know Thy Foe stacks in Social Conflicts—each separate secret contributes independently. Useful examples include:
- The target is in serious financial difficulty and desperate for allies.
- A rival or enemy of the target is nearby, and the target knows it.
- A major endeavour of the target has recently collapsed, possibly without their knowledge.[file:8]

### Know Thy Battlefield
A thorough understanding of the circumstances surrounding the conflict grants 1 Superiority. Relevant intelligence might include a threat the target is unaware of that will soon affect their situation directly, or significant unrest about to disrupt something the target has planned.[file:8]

### Know Thy Players
Inspired preparation tailored to the specific conflict may earn 1 Superiority at the GM\'s discretion. Bringing a well-armed escort to an intimidation, arriving with a conspicuously heavy purse, or carrying medicine for an ailing opponent are all examples of preparation that changes the shape of a negotiation before a word is spoken.[file:8]

**Gaining and Losing Social Superiority** Social Superiority may increase during a conflict when you win an Opposed Check using a social Skill or specialization with Levels of Success equal to or greater than the relevant Attribute bonus of your opponent. For example, winning a Manipulation Intimidation check against an opponent\'s Resilience Discipline grants 1 Superiority if your Levels of Success meet or exceed the opponent\'s Willpower bonus.[file:8]

**Spending Social Superiority** Social Superiority works differently from Combat Superiority—it is actively spent rather than passively applied, and decreases as it is used. Spending 1 Superiority grants 2 Levels of Success on a social Check. That point of Superiority is then lost. If you spend Superiority equal to or greater than the target\'s Resolve in a single Check, you also gain Advantage on that Check in addition to the bonus Levels of Success.[file:8]
