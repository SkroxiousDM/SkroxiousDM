# Core Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Action Roll | A roll used to resolve uncertain actions, including attacks, skill checks, and some spell uses. It uses 2d12, compares the result to a Challenge Threshold, and may produce success, failure, complications, or critical outcomes. [file:9] |
| Advantage | A favorable modifier to a roll. In action rolls, it is usually represented by rolling one extra d12 and discarding the highest result; some talents and special rules also grant Advantage in other ways. [file:9][file:10][file:5] |
| Attribute | A core character rating such as Strength, Constitution, Dexterity, Agility, Reason, Insight, Charisma, or Willpower. Attributes help determine derived stats and are used in many checks. [file:11] |
| Challenge Threshold | The difficulty target for an action roll. The GM sets it based on task difficulty and circumstance. [file:9] |
| Characteristic | A stat used in opposed or other checks. In the current text this term appears functionally rather than as a separate rules category. [file:9][file:8] |
| Common Test | A check used when someone is not directly opposing the character, but the outcome still matters. [file:9] |
| Complication | A result where the action succeeds or fails, but at a cost, twist, or other negative consequence. Complications can also interact with Doom or other scene resources. [file:9] |
| Condition | A named status effect that changes what a character can do or how they function, such as Staggered, Irrational, Unconscious, Frightened, Restrained, or Desperate. [file:11][file:7][file:3] |
| Critical Failure | A disastrous action roll result where both dice match on a failure. It triggers severe consequences, including Doom gain and, for attacks, a Fumble roll. [file:9] |
| Critical Hit | A strong attack result that causes a Critical Wound even if the target has not exceeded maximum Vitality. [file:7] |
| Critical Success | A powerful action roll result where both dice match on a success. It grants a strong benefit, including Fortune gain, and can improve attack results. [file:9] |
| Critical Wound | A severe injury suffered when excess damage is taken, or when an attack or effect inflicts one directly. Critical Wounds have location-based effects and can lead to death if untreated. [file:7][file:11] |
| CT | Abbreviation for Challenge Threshold. [file:9] |
| Damage | The amount of harm inflicted by an attack or effect before mitigation by armor or other defenses. Physical damage reduces Vitality; mental damage reduces Resolve. [file:7][file:11] |
| Desperate | A condition applied to NPCs when the party’s Superiority exceeds their Resolve. It can cause them to flee, surrender, or make a final attack, depending on context. [file:3][file:8] |
| Doom | A GM resource gained through certain failures, complications, and critical failures. [file:9] |
| Doom token | A unit of Doom held by the GM. [file:9] |
| Extended Check | A check resolved over multiple rolls, used for complex or time-consuming tasks with a threshold and deadline. [file:9] |
| Fate | A resource referenced as something the party may gain from a successful roll or reward cycle. [file:9] |
| Fate Point | A reward or meta-resource that may be granted by the GM in place of some success rewards. [file:9] |
| Fatigue | A cumulative condition representing exhaustion, deprivation, or despair. Each point temporarily reduces Stamina, Vitality, and Resolve by 1. [file:4] |
| Fighting | The skill used for combat actions, melee attacks, and some intimidation or force-based checks. [file:5][file:7][file:8] |
| Fortune | A player resource gained through certain critical successes or other rewards. [file:9] |
| Fortune point | A unit of Fortune held by the player. [file:9] |
| Full Rest | A 24-hour rest in a safe location. It restores all spent Stamina, all spent Resolve, all spent Power, and heals some Wounds and ability damage. [file:4] |
| GM | The gamemaster, who sets difficulties, adjudicates uncertain outcomes, and manages NPC and scene resources. [file:9][file:8] |
| Hit Location | The body part struck by an attack, usually determined by the Complication die. Locations currently listed include head, arms, legs, and body. [file:7] |
| Initiative | The order in which characters act in combat, determined by 1d12 plus Alacrity. [file:7][file:11] |
| Level of Success | A measure of how well a check succeeded or failed beyond the threshold. It can be spent on benefits such as information, faster completion, or confidence. [file:9] |
| Long Rest | An 8-hour rest that restores some Stamina, Resolve, and Vitality, and improves recovery through first aid. [file:4] |
| Opposed Test | A check where two characters roll against each other, and the higher result or higher Level of Success wins. [file:9][file:8] |
| Push | The act of rerolling a failed check at a cost of Stress, Vitality, or Resolve depending on the attribute used. [file:9][file:5] |
| Power | A magical resource used for ritual magic and investing foci. Its formula varies by magical tradition. [file:11] |
| Reaction | An action taken outside your own turn, usually in response to a trigger. Reactions cost Action and Stamina resources from the next turn or current pool depending on the rule. [file:7] |
| Resolve | A derived mental durability stat, used for resisting fear, mental damage, and other pressure. When it reaches 0, the character loses 1 Sanity and becomes Irrational. [file:11] |
| Rest | A period of recovery with different levels of intensity, including Short Rest, Long Rest, and Full Rest. [file:4] |
| Sanity | A derived mental resilience limit. Exceeding it has severe narrative and mechanical consequences determined by the GM. [file:11] |
| Skill | A trained capability tied to an attribute and used for checks. Skills can have ranks and specializations. [file:5] |
| Specialization | A focused sub-area of a skill. When it applies, it grants Advantage to the related check. [file:5] |
| Speed | A movement value used for how far a character can move on their turn. The current text references a base speed of 30 ft. unless otherwise noted. [file:7] |
| SL | Abbreviation for Success Level. [file:9][file:8] |
| Social Conflict | A structured scene where one character tries to get something from another through words, leverage, fear, negotiation, or persuasion. [file:8] |
| Social Superiority | A social momentum resource gained and spent during Social Conflict. It can improve social checks and sometimes grant Advantage when spent in sufficient quantity. [file:8] |
| Spellcaster | A keyword or character trait indicating access to magic-related talents, skills, and rules. [file:10][file:5][file:11] |
| Staggered | A condition gained when Vitality reaches 0. It reduces Alacrity, breaks Concentration, and makes the character more vulnerable to further injury. [file:11] |
| Stamina | A physical exertion resource used for movement, carrying, and other strenuous actions. It is also part of carrying capacity and recovery rules. [file:11][file:4] |
| Stamina Invested | Stamina committed to carrying gear rather than spent temporarily. It represents encumbrance and returns when the load is reduced. [file:11] |
| Stress | A cost paid when pushing a failed check and a resource or consequence tied to mental and physical strain. [file:9][file:5] |
| Superiority | A shared momentum resource representing advantage in combat or social conflict. It is gained through preparation and success, and spent for bonuses or pressure. [file:3][file:8] |
| Talent | A character feature that provides passive or active benefits, often with prerequisites. Talents can grant actions, reactions, desperate actions, or modifications to attributes and skills. [file:10] |
| Threshold | A target amount that must be reached in an extended check. [file:9] |
| Talent | A character feature that can affect attributes, derived stats, or requirements. The current text uses talents as a broad progression and formula term, but does not fully define it in one place. [file:10][file:11] |
| Vitality | A physical health resource. Damage reduces Vitality first; when it reaches 0, the character gains a Critical Wound and becomes Staggered. [file:11] |
| Critical Wound | A major injury tracked separately from Vitality. A character has a maximum number of Critical Wounds, and exceeding that limit can be fatal. [file:11] |
