## 2.1 Attributes
- Characters are defined by eight core attributes, divided into Physical and Mental categories. Each attribute is rated on a scale of 0 to 7.
- A character may not have an attribute higher than the number of Traits possessed. [start with 4 Traits from Culture, Background, and Role?]
- Through progression no attribute may exceed 7.
- At character creation the player is given 16 points to assign to the eight Attributes. [thinking this  will actually be broken up by, Culture 4pts Background 6pts, and Role 6pts.]

### 2.1.1 Physical Attributes
#### **Strength (STR):** 
Raw physical power for lifting, striking, and feats of might. 
- adds bonus to Melee attacks damage.
#### **Constitution (CON):** 
Endurance, resistance to harm, and toughness.  
#### **Dexterity (DEX):** 
Precision, speed, and hand–eye coordination. 
- used for ranged attacks
#### **Agility (AGI):** 
Reflexes, balance, and body control.
- used for melee attacks

### 2.1.2 Mental Attributes
#### **Reason (REA):** 
logic, memory, and problem–solving. 
- Bonus Languages = (REA/2)
#### **Insight (INS):** 
Awareness, perception, and intuition. 
- adds bonus to Ranged attacks damage
#### **Charisma (CHA):** 
Social presence, charm, and leadership.  
#### **Willpower (WIL):** 
Mental fortitude, determination, and force of will.  
- adds bonus to Mental attacks damage

### 2.1.3 Derived Attributes

#### **Stamina** = (STR + CON + DEX + AGI + Traits)

#### **Vitality** = (3 + CON + Traits)

#### **Evasion** = (AGI + Shield + Traits)

#### **Alacrity** = (AGI + REA + Traits)  
Characters act from highest Alacrity to lowest

#### **Resolve** = (CON + WIL + Traits)

#### **Power**
- Traditional: 2 + INS.
- Research: 2 + REA.
- Dabbling: 1 + WIL.

## 2.2 Skills
Skills represent trained capabilities tied to specific attributes and situations. Having a relevant skill grants a +1 bonus for each level of training (maximum of 5) to the associated attribute check, and a skilled character may Push a failed check to attempt it again (subject to the Push rules in section 2.3.4). In rare cases, the GM may require a specific skill to attempt an action, but most tasks can be attempted untrained.

At the GM’s discretion, possessing a relevant skill can:
- Be required to achieve a Critical Success.  
- Downgrade a Critical Failure to a normal failure.  
- Remove the need to roll, granting automatic success.

Each skill has specializations that reflect focused expertise.

### 2.2.1 Skill Specializations
A specialization is a narrow focus within a skill. For example, a character proficient in Manipulation might specialize in Deception, or a character with Academia might specialize in Philosophy.
- A character gains two specializations at character creation.
- A specialization must be tied to a skill in which the character is proficient.  
- A character cannot take the same specialization more than once.  
- When a specialization applies, it grants advantage (+1d) to the related check.  

The GM decides when a specialization applies.

### Academia
This skill covers your character's education and accumulated knowledge, as well as their ability to research unfamiliar subjects. Academia is most useful when trying to study new information or recall facts about a subject.

| Specialisation | Description |
|---|---|
| Finance | Knowledge of trade and banking and of the influence of money on politics and culture. |
| History | Knowledge of historical events and of the forces and individuals that drove those events, as well as an understanding of different historical perspectives. |
| Linguistics | The study of languages in their current and past forms, of dead languages, and of the ways in which languages change over time. A character with this focus may know up to three additional languages. *(To cast spells, requires a talent with the Spellcaster keyword.)* |
| Philosophy | The study of concepts that may underlie our perceived realities and fundamental truths, particular viewpoints on these concepts, and the history of influential philosophers. |
| Religion | The study of the gods; their manifestations; and the practices, traditions, and creatures associated with them. *(To cast spells, requires a talent with the Spellcaster keyword.)* |
| Cultures | Measures knowledge of the people and customs of the world. Can identify prominent nobles, their coat of arms, seals, or liveries, and recall information about laws and customs of a particular place or peoples. |

### Athletics
This skill measures your character's physical conditioning and athletic ability. Athletics also includes your character's attempts to move with haste.

| Specialisation | Description |
|---|---|
| Climbing | Scaling sheer surfaces such as walls and cliffs, as well as mountaineering. |
| Lifting | Lifting and moving heavy objects, as well as the conditioning necessary to make such efforts more effective. |
| Physical Training | Knowing how to train oneself and others. |
| Running | Short-distance sprinting and long-distance endurance running. |
| Swimming | Unaided swimming and diving. |

***
### Crafting
This skill incorporates your character's familiarity with techniques to fabricate goods, as well as their ability to research and develop new designs.

| Specialisation | Description |
|---|---|
| Armorsmithing | The manufacture and repair of worn armor and the forging and smithing of its component parts. |
| Cooking | Preparing meals, understanding how to use ingredients, and knowledge of food preservation techniques. |
| Tailoring | Designing, fabricating, and altering garments, from simple tunics to heavier items for weather protection, as well as leatherworking. |
| Weaponsmithing | The manufacture and repair of weaponry, including woodworking for spears and bows and metalworking for forging, shaping, and sharpening blades. |

***
### Engineering
This skill encompasses your character's technical and mechanical expertise, with an emphasis on practical applications: designing, building, and repairing devices and structures; operating equipment; and determining how to disable or destroy devices and structures.

| Specialisation | Description |
|---|---|
| Architecture | Understanding how buildings are designed and constructed, their internal and external structure, and how their mass is supported and distributed. |
| Defenses | Planning and construction of fortifications like watchtowers, palisades, trenches, and barricades. |
| Demolition | Understanding how to effectively destroy buildings, siege engines, and fortifications. |
| Infrastructure | Planning and construction of infrastructure such as roads, aqueducts, and bridges. |
| Siege Engines | Planning and construction of weaponry and tools required to perform a siege, such as siege towers, ladders, onagers, and ballistae. |

***
### Fighting
This skill covers your character's ability to fight and defend themself against attacks. It encompasses everything from punches and kicks to improvised weapons. It incorporates a combination of learned techniques, physical conditioning, and instinct, and includes a practical understanding of weaponry and combat styles.

| Specialisation | Description |
|---|---|
| Archery | Use and maintenance of bows and more modern arms like the arcuballista. |
| Melee Weapons | Fighting with a melee weapon, such as a dagger, axe, sword, or spear, as well as keeping such weapons in good order. |
| Thrown Weapons | Use of thrown weapons like javelins, throwing axes, and plumbatae, as well as weaponry like slings. |
| Unarmed | A range of unarmed techniques for striking and grappling opponents, from pankration, to fisticuffs, to simple brawling. |
| War Magic | Casting direct, damaging spells during the heat of combat. *(To cast spells, requires a talent with the Spellcaster keyword.)* |

***
### Manipulation
The Manipulation Skill is used when attempting to influence others through a mixture of speech and body language. It can be honest or dishonest, and also allows your character to spot when others are attempting to use these same techniques.

| Specialisation | Description |
|---|---|
| Charm | Methods of manipulating others through natural charisma and magnetism. |
| Deception | Methods of manipulating others to believe untruths. |
| Innuendo | The art of conveying meaning indirectly. |
| Intimidation | Inspiring fear, doubt, or hesitation, or leveraging a person's weaknesses against them. |
| Invocation | Unusual and esoteric methods of communicating with unnatural entities. Typically only available to spellcasters. *(To cast spells, requires a talent with the Spellcaster keyword.)* |
| Negotiation | Reaching agreement through conversation, possibly involving trade or payment. |
| Rhetoric | The use of language in public speaking or formal debate to inform, persuade, motivate, or convince others. |

***
### Medicine
Medicine is your character's ability to diagnose and treat conditions such as injuries and diseases. It is used for mending wounds, dealing with the symptoms of an illness, and staving off death.

| Specialisation | Description |
|---|---|
| Contagion | Understanding of diseases, their causes, the way they spread, their symptoms, and the ways in which they can be treated. |
| Faith Healing | Rites, rituals, and appropriate offerings and practices for petitioning the gods or other supernatural beings to intercede on behalf of the sick or injured. *(To cast spells, requires a talent with the Spellcaster keyword.)* |
| Field Treatment | Knowledge of how to quickly stanch bleeding and dress wounds in the field, keeping the injured alive long enough to receive more comprehensive treatment. |
| Pharmacia | Knowledge of naturally derived drugs, medicines, and toxins, including how to manufacture such substances, identify common symptoms they cause, and counteract them. |
| Surgery | Surgical, instrumental, and operative medical procedures that require cutting into a patient, extracting tissue or foreign objects, amputating limbs, or manually closing wounds. |

### Observation
This skill incorporates your character's awareness and their ability to glean information from their surroundings. It describes how observant and astute they are, how quickly they pick out important details, and how well they can ignore distractions.

| Specialisation | Description |
|---|---|
| Hearing | Auditory perception, the capacity to distinguish between different sounds, and the ability to discern information conveyed by sounds. A great asset for hunters and guards. |
| Instincts | The "sixth sense" or "gut feelings" arising from observations, as well as other extrasensory perceptions. Conveys a hunch or general feeling about a situation rather than detailed information. *(To cast spells, requires a talent with the Spellcaster keyword.)* |
| Sight | Boosts visual perception, heightening visual sensitivity when visibility is poor, and increasing the ability to spot small variations or brief movements. Valuable for scouts, navigators, and hunters. |
| Smell and Taste | The ability to detect and discern information from scents and tastes. Valuable for poisoners, physicians, food tasters, and cooks. |
| Scrutinize | Measures the ability to look around a locale and reconstruct what happened there, or glean information that isn't immediately obvious. |

### Reflexes
Reflexes represents the ability to avoid an attack or survive an accident by not being in the path of danger, as well as physical adroitness in general. Some who hone this skill learn to respond through muscle memory before they even fully recognise a threat.

| Specialisation | Description |
|---|---|
| Acrobatics | Training in gymnastic exercises, with increased capacity for gracefully jumping, rolling, and tumbling. Can be used to swiftly traverse otherwise difficult terrain. |
| Balance | Practice in keeping on one's feet despite uncertain ground, pools of slippery unguents, or treacherous walkways. |
| Dodge | The capacity for avoiding injury or accident through quick movements designed to sidestep danger. Can be used to defend against melee and ranged attacks. |

## Resilience
This skill encompasses your character's ability to resist and recover from physical and mental debilities including injury, pain, fatigue, and mental distress. It also includes withstanding the effects of poisons and diseases, as well as resisting magical effects.

| Specialisation | Description |
|---|---|
| Discipline | A character's mental durability and self-control, as well as their ability to resist and recover from confusion, fear, and supernatural effects that afflict the mind. *(To cast spells, requires a talent with the Spellcaster keyword.)* |
| Fortitude | The body's hardiness and endurance, as well as its ability to resist and recover from fatigue, pain, and injury. |
| Immunity | A character's natural ability to resist diseases and toxins, and to recover from their effects. |

***
## Stealth
Your Stealth Skill Check is made when you wish to remain unseen. It can be used to move silently, slip away undetected, hide, or remain inconspicuous even in plain sight.

| Specialisation | Description |
|---|---|
| Concealment | The ability to hide oneself, others, or objects so they will not be detected. |
| Disguise | Use of clothing, props, acting, and appropriate body language to assume the guise of another person. |
| Sneak | Moving in a hidden manner and avoiding detection. |

***
## Thievery
Your Thievery Skill Check is made when attempting to perform sleight of hand, such as picking a pocket or stealing a coin purse. It is also used for concealing small items on your person.

| Specialisation | Description |
|---|---|
| Escape Artistry | The ability to free oneself from restraints. |
| Lock Picking | Overcoming mechanical locks using picks and a torsion bar. |
| Sleight of Hand | Techniques for pilfering objects worn or carried by others, as well as making objects in hand vanish from sight. |

***
## Survival
Survival encompasses your character's knowledge of how to live and operate in the wilderness without the comforts of civilization, including securing essentials, navigating, acquiring provisions, and finding or building shelter.

| Specialisation | Description |
|---|---|
| Animal Handling | Knowledge about a range of different animals, how they behave, how to act around them, and how to train and command them. |
| Boating | Fundamental skills required to pilot, skipper, or crew a riverboat or other freshwater vessel. |
| Foraging | Gathering food from the local environment, identifying edible flora, and safely recognizing natural toxins. |
| Hunting | The ability to stalk, catch, and kill animal prey, as well as knowledge of how to prepare game. |
| Mysticism | Practices implemented in the wilds to beseech natural powers or spirits to protect the practitioner. *(To cast spells, requires a talent with the Spellcaster keyword.)* |
| Navigation | Navigating over a distance, maintaining a direction, planning a route, and enduring the physical demands of overland travel. |
| Tracking | Identifying and following tracks of a specific animal, person, or group, and determining details such as number of individuals, direction, physical state, and speed. |
| Woodcraft | Skills for wilderness living, including constructing shelter and camp facilities from local natural materials. |

## Tactics
This skill deals with your character's ability to perceive and evaluate a combat situation and devise an appropriate plan. It covers military strategy and tactics, team dynamics, leadership, and the organization of disparate groups.

| Specialisation | Description |
|---|---|
| Cavalry | Familiarity with military strategy and tactics for mounted troops. |
| Infantry | Familiarity with military strategy and infantry tactics. |
| Leadership | The ability to command and lead others, give clear orders, and maintain morale. |
| Navy | Naval strategy and tactics, from shallow to deep water and from single-boat actions to major fleet engagements. |
| Omen Reading | Reading signs in the natural world and using them to interpret the future. *(To cast spells, requires a talent with the Spellcaster keyword.)* |
| Scouting | Organizing a team to operate quietly and efficiently to stage ambushes, scout enemy positions, or perform stealthy operations in enemy territory. |
