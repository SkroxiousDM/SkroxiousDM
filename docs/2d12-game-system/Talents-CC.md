# TALENT KEYWORDS AND REQUIREMENTS
All talents are labeled with one or more keywords, which group similar talents together. Keywords are labels that usually signify which skill, rule, or group a talent most often applies to. Other keywords require a character to do something, such as spend a Fortune point, to use a talent. Still others convey a certain status or capability: the Spellcaster keyword lets a character cast spells, for instance.

Certain talents also have requirements, which limit those talents’ access to characters with a particular archetype, caste, culture, related talent, or similar. If a keyword description lists no requirements, then none apply.

Categories that keywords and requirements most commonly make use of are described in this section.
## Keyword Categories
- Fortune: The use of the talent involves spending a Fortune point.
- Movement: A talent with the Movement keyword relates to a character’s movement but does not necessarily require a Movement minor action.
- Skill: Talents with a skill as their keyword affect skill tests that use that skill. “<Skill>” denotes a talent that can be used with a single specific skill, one you choose when you take the talent: replace “<Skill>” with the name of the skill you choose.
- Spellcaster: A talent with the Spellcaster keyword, called a spellcaster talent, allows a character to cast spells. A character may have only one talent with the Spellcaster keyword.
- Weird: A talent with the Weird keyword represents something unusual, unnatural, or inexplicable. Many of these talents come with some drawback or quirk that sets a character apart as strange. After character creation, Weird talents may only be selected with the GM’s permission.
## Requirement Categories
- Advanced: A character must have 3 or more ranks in the associated skill to choose a talent with the Advanced requirement.
- Archetype: Only characters with the listed archetype can choose a talent with an archetype requirement. Most archetype talents are listed with their related archetype see Step 2: Choose Archetype on page 96).
- Caste: Only characters with the listed caste can choose a talent with a caste requirement. Most caste talents are listed with their related caste (see Castes on page 123).
- Culture: Only characters with the listed culture can choose a talent with a culture requirement. Most culture talents are listed with their related culture (see Cultures on page 124).
- Talent: Only characters with the talent in its Requirement line can choose a talent with a talent requirement. Most talents with this type of requirement are part of a talent tree and appear in Archetype Specializations on page 110.

## COMMON TALENTS
### ADVISOR
#### Keywords: <Skill>
You’ve got a knack for guiding others through problems. When you select this talent, choose a single skill. Whenever you assist an ally and you use that skill, the ally you assist may re-roll a single d20 in their dice pool.
### BOLD
#### Keywords: <Skill>
When you take calculated risks, you tend to succeed more often than seems reasonable. When you select this talent, choose a single skill. When you make a skill test using that skill, and you buy additional d20s by generating Threat for the GM’s Threat pool, you may re-roll a single d20 in that dice pool.
### CAUTIOUS
#### Keywords: <Skill>
You’re patient and circumspect, acting only when the odds are in your favor. When you select this talent, choose a single skill. When you make a skill test using that skill, and you buy additional d20s by spending Momentum, you may re-roll a single d20 in that dice pool.
### COLLABORATION
#### Keywords: <Skill>
Requirements: Advanced
You’ve coached your allies to capitalize on your expertise, and that effort has paid off. When you select this talent, choose a single skill in which you have 3 or more ranks. Whenever an ally makes a skill test using that skill, and you can communicate with them, you may spend 2 Momentum to allow them to use your ranks in that skill instead of their own, as well as one of your focuses instead of their own (if applicable).
## Fortune
### COOL UNDER PRESSURE
#### Keywords: <Skill>, Fortune
When the situation gets tough, you take a deep breath and get the job done. When you select this talent, choose a single skill. When you make a skill test using that skill, you may spend a Fortune point to automatically pass that skill test, but you generate
no Momentum.
### PRODIGIALIS FORTUNA
#### Keywords: Fortune
The gods who control fate are entertained by you, casting you about on the tides of luck both good and ill. Whenever you spend a Fortune point, you may roll 1 . If you roll an effect , you immediately regain that Fortune point. If you roll a blank, however, you immediately generate 3 Threat for the GM’s Threat pool.
### SKIN OF YOUR TEETH
Keywords: Fortune
You have a knack for (just barely) avoiding the worst injuries. Once per scene, you may spend a Fortune point when you are dealt an injury in order to avoid suffering that injury.
## Academia
### APPLIED KNOWLEDGE
#### Keywords: Academia
The knowledge you have accumulated gives you bursts of insight to deal with unexpected challenges. Once per scene, you may use Academia in place of another skill when you make a skill test, and you are counted as having a focus for that test.
### MAGISTER’S FAVORITE
#### Keywords: Academia
You paid attention to your tutor, were extensively self-taught, or both. You have constantly sought out knowledge beyond the scope of your minimum necessary training. If you pass an Academia-based skill test, you gain +1 bonus Momentum. Bonus Momentum may not be saved.
### PLATONIST
#### Keywords: Academia
You are skilled at understanding the divide between the utilitarian external world and the internal world of the mind. You gain additional Courage resistance equal to your ranks in Academia. This does not stack with other talents that grant extra Courage resistance.
### SAVANT
#### Keywords: Academia
You display an uncommon aptitude. Choose any two skills to which your archetype provided bonus ranks. For each of those skills, you may either increase its ranks by +1 or select a focus for it.

## Athletics
### LUDI CHAMPION
#### Keywords: Athletics
You have competed, and prevailed, in Rome’s annual athletic
competition or in its equivalent in another culture. When you make
an Athletics-based skill test to run, ride, or swim, ignore the
first complication you roll.
### MARATHON RUNNER
#### Keywords: Athletics
When you are running, you always maintain a reserve of energy to
help you cross the finish line. On your turn, you may take the Rush
major action even if you have already taken the Movement
minor action.
### SCULPTED FROM MARBLE
#### Keywords: Athletics
You are a physically remarkable specimen. When you make an
Athletics-based skill test to lift, run, throw, or swim, you may
re-roll any number of dice on the test.
## Crafting
### FIELD REPAIR
#### Keywords: Crafting
You are adept at repairing items in the field with suboptimal
materials. When you make an Insight + Crafting test to repair
a damaged item (such as armor or a weapon) in the field, you
may generate 2 Threat for the GM’s Threat pool to reduce the
test’s difficulty by 1, to a minimum of 1. The repaired item gains
the Field Repairs truth.
### SCAVENGER
#### Keywords: Crafting
You have a knack for finding supplies. By spending half an hour
scrounging for useful ingredients and passing an Insight
+ Crafting test with a difficulty of 1, you can gather enough
ingredients to replenish the resources of a crafting tools skill kit.
## Engineering
### DEMOLITIONIST
#### Keywords: Engineering
You’ve got a knack for breaking things. When you make a skill
test to attack an object, structure, or stationary vehicle, you
may use your Engineering skill instead of Fighting, and you
may gain bonus from Reason instead of Brawn or Insight.
When you inflict damage on such a target, you may spend 2
Momentum to take any number of rolled up to your ranks
in Engineering and replace each with an effect result.
### QUALITY CRAFTING
#### Keywords: Engineering
The objects, vehicles, and structures you build are a cut above the rest
and can withstand much more wear and tear. When you construct an
object, its maximum stress increases by +2, and the total number
of injuries it can sustain increases by +1. (see Damaging Objects on
page 34, for more information on damaging objects.)
### THE TOOL FOR THE JOB
#### Keywords: Engineering
You’re used to putting in hard work and getting the job done. When
you make an Engineering-based skill test as part of an extended
test, the damage you roll gains the Piercing 1 damage effect.
## Fighting
### AGGRESSIVE
#### Keywords: Fighting
When you make an attack with a melee weapon, you may
reduce the Momentum cost of the first d20 you buy to 0. If
you do so, the difficulty of attacks targeting you is reduced by
1 until the start of your next turn. A character with this talent
cannot also gain the Defensive talent.
### DEFENSIVE
#### Keywords: Fighting
You’re especially good at avoiding harm. Select melee attacks or
ranged attacks. When you are targeted by the selected form of
attack, the attack’s difficulty increases by +1. This talent may be
selected two times, once for each form of attack. A character
with this talent cannot also gain the Aggressive talent.
### SKIRMISHER
#### Keywords: Fighting
One per round when an enemy character misses you with a melee
or ranged attack, you may generate 2 Threat for the GM’s Threat
pool and, as a free action, move to anywhere within Medium
range. When you do so, you gain +2 Cover resistance against melee
and ranged attacks until the start of your next turn.
### STEADY AIM
#### Keywords: Fighting
Your attacks with ranged weapons are deadly accurate. When you
take the Aim minor action with a ranged weapon, you may
re-roll 2d20 on your attack roll that turn, rather than 1d20.

##Medicine
### ANATOMIST
#### Keywords: Medicine
Your work with injured creatures has given you a working knowledge of how best to injure them. When you attack a human target, or an animal or creature that you have previously dissected, you can land your strikes in vulnerable areas. You can use Medicine instead of Fighting for attacks targeting such creatures and can add the Vicious damage effect to the attacks.
### COMBAT MEDICUS
#### Keywords: Medicine
You know the basics of treating wounds in the field. When you use the Stabilize major action, you may re-roll 1d20 from your Coordination + Medicine test. If you pass the test, you may also remove one condition (see Conditions on page 44).
##Observation
### KEEN EYED
#### Keywords: Observation
Your eyesight is as sharp as an eagle’s. The increased difficulty of your Perception-based skill tests for zones beyond Close is reduced by 1 (see Distance and Perception on page 31). In addition, when you use a ranged weapon, its normal range and the next higher zone are both considered its effective range. For example, a weapon with an effective range of medium can now be effectively used at both medium and long ranges without an increase in difficulty normally imposed by attacking outside a weapon’s effective range.
### PREDATOR’S SENSES
#### Keywords: Observation
Your senses are as sharp as those of a wolf on the hunt. While you are conscious, enemies cannot receive the benefits of a surprise attack against you (see Surprise Attack on page 158). Additionally, the gamemaster must spend +1 Threat to have NPCs act first in a combat encounter.
### SUSPICIOUS
#### Keywords: Observation
You are suspicious of everything and everyone. You treat every suspicious character (or suspect thicket of brambles) like it might be a threat to your life. Before your turn in the first round of combat, the difficulty of any hostile character’s physical or mental attack targeting you increases by +1.
##Persuasion
### IMPOSING PRESENCE
#### Keywords: Persuasion
It’s always been easy for you to intimidate others. You can use a melee weapon to make a mental attack with a Gravitas + Persuasion skill test. The attack deals mental damage equal to your weapon’s + your bonus based on your Will, with your choice of the Piercing 1 or Stun damage effect. You do not need the Fearsome X special rule to make this mental attack.
### LINGUA ARGENTEA
#### Keywords: Persuasion
You’re blessed with a silver tongue, allowing you to influence people to adopt your views quickly and effectively. When you make a Persuasion-based skill test to sway someone to your way of thinking or haggle over the price of an item, the test’s difficulty is reduced by 1.
##Resilience
### IRON HIDE
#### Keywords: Resilience
You possess great fortitude and can bear horrible injuries without showing it. For the purpose of increasing your complication range, you ignore the first injury you are suffering from. In addition, you are defeated after suffering 4 injuries instead of 3. The GM determines if you are dying or irrational.
### MITHRADATIC
#### Keywords: Resilience
You are either naturally resistant to toxins or have become inured to them through constant exposure. When you need to make a Resilience-based skill test to avoid the effects of a poison, toxin, or drug, the difficulty is reduced by 1. If this reduces the difficulty to 0, it eliminates the need for a skill test.
### SECOND WIND
#### Keywords: Resilience
When you take the Catch Breath major action, roll 1 per your ranks in Resilience. For each effect result this generates, you may remove 1 additional point of stress.
##Stealth
### FADE AWAY
#### Keywords: Stealth
Once per scene as a minor action, you may generate 2 Threat for the GM’s Threat pool to slip from sight. If you do, you cannot be the target of an attack (other than attacks with the Area damage effect) until the start of your next turn. If you are in heavy cover, you do not need to generate Threat to use this talent.
### LOW PROFILE
Keywords: Stealth
When you gain Cover resistance, the total Cover resistance gained increases by +1.
##Survival
### BESTIARIUS
#### Keywords: Survival
You aren’t alone in the wilderness. You have a nonsapient
companion creature that serves as a friend and ally
in dangerous times. The companion creature has the
Companion Creature profile included in this talent entry
or one of the profiles in Appendix B: Companion Creatures
on page 211. Your companion creature is treated as an
allied non-player character under your command. When
you forage for supplies, your companion looks after
itself. If your companion is ever slain by enemy action or
misfortune, you immediately gain a nonrefreshing Fortune
point and have a choice between retraining this talent (and
getting a new companion) or replacing this talent with a
different talent.
### UNTRACEABLE
#### Keywords: Survival
You know how to move through the wilderness without leaving
a trace of your passage. When you are traveling alone or
leading a group, you can increase the difficulty of skill tests
to track your movements by half your ranks in Survival,
rounding down.
##Tactics
### COORDINATOR
#### Keywords: Tactics
You are proficient in coordinating the actions of a group working in
unison. Anytime you participate in a skill test being made with
assistance—even if you are not the assisted skill test’s leader—
the player of each assisting or leading character may choose to
re-roll 1d20 but must accept the new result.
### INCONSPICUOUS
#### Keywords: Tactics
You know how to keep a low profile and avoid suspicion. When your
weapons or equipment generate Threat, you reduce the total
Threat by 1.
Note that if a relic or profane item has a special rule that allows it
to generate additional Threat, you cannot reduce the Threat of that
object below 1.
### RALLYING CRY
#### Keywords: Tactics
With a confident yell, you can stop your comrades from quaking in
their caligae (sandal-style hobnailed boots). To use this talent, roll a
number of equal to your ranks in Tactics. Instead of inflicting
stress on your stress track, each point of stress generated by the
roll lets you or an ally within Close range remove 1 stress. Each
roll of lets you remove 2 stress instead of 1. You may apportion
the total points of stress removed among yourself and your allies
within Close range in any way you choose.

## Weird
### BIZARRE INSIGHT
#### Keywords: Weird
Your mind often shows you glimpses of things you couldn’t otherwise know. Once per scene, you may generate 1 Threat for the GM’s Threat Pool to Obtain Information (ask the GM a question; see Spending Momentum on page 23) without passing a skill test.
### FOREBODING SURVIVAL
#### Keywords: Weird
Every so often, it feels like you narrowly avoid disaster thanks to an unknown guiding hand. How you avoid these fates is unknown, but bad luck seems to befall those around you. Once per session when you are dealt an injury, you may generate 3 Threat for the GM’s Threat pool in order to avoid suffering that injury. At the GM’s discretion, you may be offered the chance to avoid other misfortune in exchange for generating 3 Threat as well.
### MYSTICAL POWER
#### Keywords: Weird
You may select this talent only if you are a spellcaster. You have delved deep into the immortal mysteries of the Mythos and the secrets of time and have returned with a greater aptitude for magical endeavors. You gain an additional personal truth: Glimpsed What Mortals Should Not Know or another truth that reflects the toll this knowledge has taken. When you cast a spell, you may increase your power rating by +2 . If you do so, each of your allies within Close range suffers 1 mental stress for each effect you roll as part of the spell’s cost, as they’re exposed to the unearthly secrets you have learned.
### NUMB TO THE HORRORS
#### Keywords: Weird
For some reason, blood, death, and violence don’t affect you mentally. You increase your Courage resistance by +6, but you cannot add any other source of Courage resistance. In addition, you may re-roll 1d20 on any skill test to determine whether you gain a mental scar. However, others find your distant manner off-putting, which increases the complication range of all Persuasion-based skill tests you make by +1.
### OCCULT DABBLER
#### Keywords: Spellcaster, Weird
You’ve dabbled with occult forces beyond your comprehension. You are now a spellcaster of the dabbling spellcaster type, as described in Chapter 4: Magic on page 58).
