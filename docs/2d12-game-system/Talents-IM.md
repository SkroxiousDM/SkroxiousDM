Here is the full Talents chapter rewritten for a human-centric pulp fantasy setting spanning the late Roman Empire through the Carolingian era, with consistent voice, reskinned flavour, and all sci-fi references removed and replaced with period-appropriate equivalents.

***

## Chapter 4: Traits

Attributes represent raw potential. Skills represent training and expertise. **Traits** are distinct from both—they are the tricks, hard-won habits, and extraordinary capabilities that set a hero apart from the common man.

Characters gain Traits during character creation through their Role, and potentially through their Culture and Background. After character creation, a character may spend **100 XP** to purchase a new Trait, provided they meet its Requirements.

### Requirements

Some Traits have Requirements that must be met before purchase. Losing the ability to meet a Requirement means losing the Trait.

Requirements typically include:
- An attribute above or below a certain value, such as AGI 4 or less than 2 REA.
- A number of advances in a skill, such as 2 advances in Fighting.
- A number of advances in a specialization, such as 2 advances in Fighting (Melee Weapons).
- The absence of advances in a specific skill or specialization.
- Some Traits may only be taken at character creation.
- Some Traits prevent or require taking other Traits.

***

### Acute Sense

One of your primary senses is exceptionally sharp, allowing you to detect what others miss entirely. You may make Observation tests to perceive details normally beyond mortal notice—spotting a lone rider at extreme distance, detecting a near-odourless poison in food or drink, hearing a hidden man's breath through a closed door, identifying cloth quality by touch, or knowing by taste that two batches of wine come from different vineyards.

***

### Adrenaline

When your life is on the line, you move faster than you thought possible. If you have a Critical Wound, your Speed increases by one step until the end of the encounter, and you have Advantage on Athletics tests made during a Pursuit or while Fleeing.

***

### Agile Attacks

*Requirements: AGI 4, 2 advances in Fighting*

Speed and precision serve you better than brute force. You may use your Agility Bonus instead of your Strength Bonus when calculating damage for melee weapons with the Subtle trait.

***

### Air of Authority

*Requirement: 2 advances in Manipulation*

You carry yourself with the bearing of someone accustomed to being obeyed. The first time you encounter a new group, you may make a Challenging Manipulation or Rapport test. On a success, you gain +1 Influence with that group until the end of the session.

***

### Ambidextrous

You use both hands with equal facility and suffer no penalty for actions performed with your off hand. You may wield a one-handed melee weapon and a thrown weapon or short blade simultaneously without penalty to either.

***

### Applied Anatomy

*Requirement: 2 advances in Medicine*

Your knowledge of the body gives you a clear understanding of how injuries occur—and how to inflict them deliberately. When you deal a Critical Hit or Critical Wound, you may increase its rolled severity by your number of advances in Medicine.

***

### Artistic

You have a natural gift for one of the arts. This Trait may be taken multiple times; each time, choose one of the following:

- **Literature:** +2 SL to any test involving reading or writing, such as drafting a compelling legal brief or identifying which classical texts are referenced in an encoded message.
- **Painting:** +2 SL to any test involving painting or drawing, such as rendering a likeness of a suspect or identifying the hand of a forger.
- **Music:** +2 SL to any test involving singing or instrumental performance, such as leading a liturgical chorus or identifying a traveller's homeland from a tune they hum.
- **Theatre:** +2 SL to any test involving acting or formal performance, such as impersonating an official or recognizing that someone's uniform was not made for them.

***

### Attentive Assistant

You render aid efficiently and willingly, even when the task falls outside your training. You may use the Help Action to assist a character even if you have no advances in the skill being tested. If you do have an advance, the recipient gains +1 SL on the test.

***

### Battle Rage

You can work yourself into a furious killing state. As a Free Action, make a Challenging Willpower test to enter a rage. While raging, your Strength Bonus increases by +1 and you are immune to the Frightened condition. On your turn, you must move toward the nearest visible enemy and engage them in melee. The rage ends when all visible enemies are defeated or when you suffer the Stunned or Unconscious condition.

This Trait may be taken multiple times. Each time after the first, choose one:

- **Controlled Fury:** You may make a Difficult Resilience (Discipline) test as an Action to end your rage voluntarily.
- **Fearless Rage:** Whenever you would become Frightened, you may immediately enter a rage instead.
- **Relentless Momentum:** Once per turn, when you kill an enemy while raging, you may immediately Move or take one additional Action.

***

### Bone Breaker

*Requirement: 2 advances in a Medicine specialization*

Your anatomical knowledge allows you to find and exploit structural weaknesses with clinical precision. As an Action, you may break a bone of a Restrained target for whom you have the relevant Medicine specialization. Choose a Hit Location: it gains a Broken Bone (Minor) condition. If it already has a Broken Bone (Minor), it becomes a Broken Bone (Major) instead.

***

### Brawler's Cunning

*Requirement: 2 advances in Fighting (Unarmed)*

You are well-versed in underhanded tactics. When you deal damage with a melee attack, you may choose to inflict the Blinded or Deafened condition. If you succeed by +3 SL or more, you may inflict the Stunned condition instead.

***

### Briber

*Requirement: 2 advances in Observation (Instincts)*

You have an instinct for who can be bought, and for what price. You have Advantage on Observation (Instincts) tests to gauge whether someone is open to a bribe. Additionally, you reduce the cost of any bribe by 10% for each advance you have in Rapport (Negotiation).

***

### Bulwark

*Requirement: 3 advances in Reflexes*

You are skilled at protecting others with your shield. When you take the Defend Action while carrying a shield, you may choose to extend your defence to one ally within Immediate Range, or to both that ally and the zone you currently occupy.

***

### Burglar

*Requirements: 1 advance in each of Athletics and Stealth*

Breaking and entering comes naturally to you. You have Advantage on tests related to unlawful entry, including climbing building exteriors, forcing or picking locks, and opening secured strongboxes or doors without keys.

***

### Chirurgeon

*Requirement: 2 advances in Medicine*

You possess the tools and training to perform surgery in the field. You may perform amputations and treat Critical Wounds beyond the capability of untrained healers. You also have Advantage when treating Critical Wounds during an encounter.

***

### Combat Master

*Requirement: 2 advances in Fighting*

Your experience allows you to keep lesser fighters off-balance and uncertain. You count as two combatants for the purpose of determining whether one side outnumbers another in melee.

***

### Condemn the Witch

*Requirements: WIL 4, Spellcaster Trait*

Denying a sorcerer access to their power is not enough for you—you turn it back against them. When you successfully use Deny the Witch to prevent a spellcaster from manifesting a power, that caster suffers Wounds equal to the power's difficulty rating.

***

### Contortionist

You can bend and twist your body into remarkably small spaces, or slip restraints that would hold others fast. You may fit into unusually cramped passages. You also have Advantage on tests to resist or escape the Restrained condition or a grapple.

***

### Counter Attack

*Requirement: 3 advances in Fighting*

Your defence and offence are inseparable. When you are the target of a melee attack and win the Opposed Fighting test, you may choose to deal damage to your attacker as though you had made the attack yourself.

This Trait may be taken multiple times. You may Counter Attack once per round for each time you have taken this Trait.

***

### Coup de Grâce

*Requirement: 3 advances in Reflexes*

You know how to press an overwhelming advantage to a swift and decisive conclusion. If you have 2 or more Superiority, you may take 2 turns in a row, sacrificing your turn next round to do so.

***

### Dealmaker

*Requirements: 1 advance in each of Observation (Instincts) and Rapport (Negotiation)*

You know how to read a negotiation and close it to your benefit. During any negotiation, make an Observation (Instincts) test. On a success, gain one of the following:

- You learn exactly what the target would accept as a bribe, whether coin, goods, or information.
- You reduce the cost of anything you purchase from the target by 10%.
- You increase the price you receive for anything you sell to the target by 10%.

***

### Deadshot

*Requirement: 1 advance in Fighting (Archery) or Fighting (Thrown Weapons)*

When you take time to aim before loosing, the benefits of your training show clearly. When you Aim and make a ranged attack, you gain +1 SL. If you take this Trait again, you also have Advantage on tests to Target a specific Location when aiming.

***

### Devoted Servant

You have a particular aptitude for honest toil. Choose one entry from the Devoted Servant table below. You have Advantage on all tests related to that area of expertise.

| Vocation | Description | Tests |
|---|---|---|
| Agrarian | You are skilled in farming, herding, or fishing, and know how to work the land or water for sustenance. | Tests related to food production or identifying the provenance of food. |
| Craftsman | You are a master of a specific craft—choose one at creation, such as smithing, weaving, or carpentry. | Tests related to your chosen craft, including appraising or identifying the work of others. |
| Cook | You can prepare nourishing and palatable meals from even poor ingredients. | Tests to prepare food, including detecting poison by taste or smell. |
| Entertainer | You are a skilled performer—acrobat, storyteller, musician, or similar—in service to lords and their guests. | Tests to entertain an audience, or to evaluate another performer's training and skill. |
| Hunter | You are self-sufficient in the wilds. Choose an environment at creation, such as forest, moorland, or river delta. | Tests to set traps or identify animals; you may provide food for a number of people equal to your PER score when in your chosen environment. |
| Mason | You are an expert builder and architect, trained in the methods of Roman, Byzantine, or Frankish construction. | Tests to determine a building's layout, find hidden rooms, or assess structural stability. |
| Servant | You are trained in running a noble household, managing staff, and anticipating the needs of your betters. | Tests related to domestic management, or recognizing the signs of disorder or infiltration in a household. |
| Steward | You manage the logistics of an estate, garrison, or camp: provisions, record-keeping, and organization. | Tests related to supply, record-keeping, and identifying waste or theft within an organization. |

***

### Directed Strike

*Requirement: 3 advances in Fighting*

You aim your blows with deliberate precision. You do not suffer Disadvantage when using the Target Location Action with a melee weapon.

***

### Disarm

*Requirement: 2 advances in Fighting*

A precise blow or a well-timed flick of the wrist can send an enemy's weapon flying. When you make a melee attack against a target your size or smaller, you may choose to disarm rather than deal damage. On a success, the target drops a held item, which lands in a random adjacent zone. If you win by +2 SL, you choose the zone; if you win by +4 SL, you may take the item yourself.

***

### Distracting

You know how to disrupt, unsettle, and madden opponents with an outburst—an insult, a sudden shout, or simply an infuriating display of poor timing. Once per encounter, you may interrupt another individual making a test, imposing Disadvantage. In combat, this Trait is used as a Reaction.

If you take this Trait a second time, choose one:

- **Centre of Attention:** When within Immediate Range of a non-ally, you may impose Disadvantage on tests that target makes toward anyone other than you.
- **Stunning Interjection:** Once per encounter when you use this Trait, your target is Stunned (Minor) until the end of their next turn.

***

### Drilled

*Requirement: 1 advance in Resilience (Discipline)*

You have faced danger alongside trained warriors and know how to hold the line. You have Advantage on tests to resist the Frightened condition, and gain +1 SL to Resilience (Discipline) tests for each ally with the Drilled Trait in the same zone.

If another party member also has Drilled, both of you may purchase the Trait again to gain the following:

- **Shield Wall:** When in the same zone as another party member with Drilled, you may act immediately after them in Initiative and gain +1 SL to all tests that round.

***

### Dual Wielder

*Requirements: Ambidextrous, 2 advances in Fighting (Melee Weapons)*

You have mastered the art of fighting with a weapon in each hand. You do not suffer Disadvantage when attacking with two melee weapons simultaneously.

***

### Duellist

*Requirement: 2 advances in Fighting (Melee Weapons)*

In single combat with a blade, you are a formidable opponent. You treat all one-handed melee weapons that lack the Subtle trait as having the Defensive trait.

This Trait may be taken multiple times. Each time after the first, choose one:

- **Duellist Feint:** When you successfully attack with a one-handed melee weapon, you may choose to deal no damage and instead impose Disadvantage on the target's attacks against you until the end of your next turn.
- **Duellist Guard:** When you make an attack with a one-handed melee weapon, you may choose to make that attack with Disadvantage in order to simultaneously take the Dodge Action.

***

### Eidetic Memory

You can recall what you have seen with extraordinary clarity and fidelity. Once you observe something, even briefly, you can reproduce it from memory with near-perfect accuracy. You do not need to make tests to recall visual details.

***

### Ever Vigilant

*Requirement: 2 advances in Observation*

Your senses are never entirely at rest. You gain +1 SL on tests to detect hidden enemies, and you suffer no penalties from the Unconscious or Blinded conditions while sleeping. Additionally, whenever the party leaves a location containing an important clue or object they did not examine, the GM may make a secret Observation test on your behalf. On a success, the GM may quietly reveal the detail to you.

***

### Exploit Vulnerability

*Requirement: 2 advances in Observation (Instincts)*

You turn knowledge of your enemies into a decisive edge. If you spend time studying a target before combat, you may choose the Hit Location of your first successful attack against them.

***

### False Retreat

*Requirement: 3 advances in Manipulation (Deception)*

You know how to use feigned cowardice as a tactical tool. As an Action, you may pretend to flee. Reduce Superiority by 1 and remove yourself from the encounter. At the start of your next turn, you may reappear at any location within Long Range of your previous position, hidden from enemies. When you re-enter the encounter, Superiority increases by 1.

***

### Faithful

*Requirements: Must be a member of a recognized religious order, have +2 Influence with a church, or have 2 advances in Academia (Religion).*

Your faith is not merely belief—it is a living force. Once per session, you may add your Willpower Bonus in SL to a test directly related to your faith. Examples include:

- Invoking divine judgement during the interrogation of an accused heretic or oathbreaker.
- Crying out a battle blessing as you strike down a creature of darkness, adding SL to your attack.
- Praying for guidance when searching for a sacred text or holy relic, adding SL to your Academia (Religion) test.

> **Saints and Miracles**
> With the GM's permission, a character who has displayed extraordinary and consistent faith may take the Faithful Trait a second time. Such characters begin to manifest what witnesses call miracles—healings, protections, or feats that defy ordinary explanation. These abilities are powerful and rare, and the GM should be prepared for them to draw significant attention: from the devout, from the envious, and from forces that would prefer such things did not exist.
>
> **Spending Fate.** You may spend a Fate Point to channel your faith as a minor miracle:
> - As an Action, you may touch a person and heal Wounds equal to 1 + your Willpower Bonus.
> - When an ally within sight suffers a condition, you may allow them to ignore that condition until the end of the encounter.
> - When a spellcaster within Long Range attempts to manifest a power, you may subtract SL equal to your Willpower Bonus from their casting test.
>
> **Burning Fate.** You may burn a Fate Point to perform one great and inexplicable act—restoring the apparently dead, holding back a collapsing structure, or calming a panicked crowd by presence alone. If you have no Fate remaining, you may still perform one final act of this kind, at the cost of your life.

***

### Familiar Terrain

You know the land of your upbringing the way others know their own face. Choose an environment below; within that environment, you have Advantage on Stealth tests and gain +1 Armour from Cover beyond what cover normally provides. This Trait may be taken multiple times, choosing a new environment each time.

- **Agricultural:** Farmed fields, orchards, pastures, fishing villages.
- **Forest:** Deep woodland, managed hunting grounds, ancient groves.
- **Highlands:** Mountains, hill country, exposed moorland.
- **Maritime:** Coastlines, harbours, river deltas, shallow water.
- **Ruins:** Abandoned settlements, derelict forts, overgrown Roman infrastructure.
- **Settlement:** Towns, cities, marketplaces, and densely populated urban areas.
- **Wasteland:** Marshes, tundra, desert, and other uninhabited wilderness.
- **War Zone:** Active battlefields, siege works, military encampments, and recently contested ground.

***

### Fated

*Requirement: Character creation only*

Fortune favours you with unusual consistency, whether by luck, divine grace, or some purpose you have yet to understand. You gain 1 additional Fate Point. This Trait may only be taken once.

***

### Field Surgeon

*Requirement: 2 advances in Medicine*

Most healers need quiet, clean conditions to work. You do not. When making a Medicine test to heal a character during or immediately after a crisis, the Difficulty cannot be harder than Challenging.

***

### Flagellant

Your devotion to your faith has stripped away much of your concern for your own flesh. You endure suffering willingly, even gratefully, as a form of worship. You have Advantage on tests to resist the effects of pain or interrogation. Additionally, whenever you suffer a Critical Wound, you may make a Challenging Academia (Religion) test. On a success, you may ignore that Critical Wound's effects for a number of rounds equal to your Willpower Bonus, your faith sustaining you where your body would otherwise fail. At the end of this duration, the full effects apply.

***

### Flanking Shot

*Requirement: 2 advances in Fighting (Archery or Thrown Weapons)*

You know how to exploit the distraction created by your allies. When you make a ranged attack targeting an enemy in the same zone as a visible ally, the cover your target benefits from is reduced by 2 Armour.

***

### Forger

*Requirements: 2 advances in each of Academia (Linguistics) and Academia (Cultures or Law)*

You can produce convincing documentation to claim authority or influence over a specific institution. The type of test required depends on the institution you are forging credentials for—the GM determines this based on the target faction. The process takes a full day.

The Difficulty of the test determines the Influence the forgery grants:
- Difficult: +1 Influence
- Hard: +2 Influence
- Very Hard: +3 Influence

Track the SL of your test. Anyone scrutinizing the document must win an Opposed Reason (Evaluation) test against your original SL to identify the forgery.

***

### Gallows Humour

*Requirement: 2 advances in Rapport (Charm)*

You find dark comedy in dire circumstances and use it to pull allies back from the edge. As an Action, you may make a Challenging Rapport (Charm) test to benefit an ally in one of the following ways:

- Remove the Fatigued or Frightened condition.
- Remove the Stunned condition.
- If the target is Dying, they do not perish at the end of the round and instead survive for 1 + SL additional rounds.

***

### Guardian

*Requirement: 3 advances in Reflexes*

You are fast enough to place yourself between an ally and harm. At the start of combat, as a Free Action, designate one allied character within Medium Range as your ward. If that character is attacked and you are within Medium Range, you may immediately move to Defend them. If doing so breaks your engagement, opponents have Disadvantage on their melee attack.

***

### Hatred

You carry a deep and consuming hatred for a particular group. Choose the object of your hatred—heathens, slavers, a specific faction, a particular kind of enemy. Whenever you encounter them, make a Difficult Resilience (Discipline) test. If you fail, you have Advantage on Resilience (Fear) tests against them and gain +1 SL on attack tests targeting them, but you must pursue and destroy them by the most direct means available. You may repeat the Discipline test at the end of each of your turns to regain control. The effect ends when you fall unconscious or all visible targets of your hatred are dead.

***

### Hit and Run

*Requirement: 3 advances in Reflexes (Acrobatics)*

When you wound an enemy, you use the moment to reposition. As a Free Action, whenever you deal one or more Wounds or Critical Wounds, you may make a Reflexes (Acrobatics) test to Disengage and move to an adjacent zone.

***

### Holdout

You are expert at concealing a small item on your person—inside your clothing, within a bandage wrapping, or in a hidden pocket sewn into your cloak. You may conceal one item no larger than the palm of your hand. Any search of your person requires a Hard Observation (Sight or Touch) or Thievery (Sleight of Hand) test to discover it.

***

### Icon Bearer

*Requirements: 1 advance in Academia (Religion) and 1 advance in Manipulation (Rhetoric or Invocation)*

When you raise a holy icon—a cross, a saint's relic, a consecrated banner—nearby allies are heartened. While you hold the icon aloft, all allies within Medium Range who can see you gain the icon's listed benefit.

***

### Inheritance

*Requirement: Character creation, or with GM permission*

Your birth or early circumstances have placed you within a lineage of significance—a noble house, an important military family, a merchant dynasty, or a religious office. You gain +1 Influence with an appropriate faction, provided you continue to honour the obligations of that lineage. With the GM's permission, you may also begin play with additional wealth, property, or connections reflecting your station.

***

### Inspiring Presence

*Requirement: 2 advances in Manipulation (Rhetoric or Leadership)*

Your bearing and resolve fill those around you with courage. Allies in your zone who can see you gain Advantage on Resilience (Discipline) tests.

***

### Lawbringer

*Requirement: 2 advances in Academia (Law or Cultures)*

You know the codes, precedents, and customs of your land well enough to wield them as weapons. When making a Manipulation or Rapport test against a target you know has broken a recognised law or custom, you gain +SL equal to your advances in the relevant Academia specialization.

***

### Lickspittle

You are a masterful flatterer—a sycophant of extraordinary skill who knows exactly what a superior wants to hear, and how to say it. You have Advantage on any test made to ingratiate yourself with someone of higher social standing.

***

### Martyr

*Requirement: 2 advances in Resilience (Fortitude)*

You are unafraid to bleed in service to your cause. If you have at least one untreated Critical Wound received in that service, you gain +SL on tests equal to your Willpower Bonus. Only tests made to directly confront enemies may benefit from this bonus.

***

### Mental Fortress

*Requirement: 2 advances in Resilience (Discipline)*

Your mind is difficult to reach and harder to hold. You cannot be read by magical means, and you reduce any damage you suffer from spells or supernatural effects by your Willpower Bonus.

***

### Mimic

*Requirements: 2 advances in each of Observation (Hearing) and Manipulation (Deception)*

After listening to someone speak for at least one hour, you can reproduce their voice and speech patterns convincingly. This Trait does not allow you to speak languages you do not know.

***

### Overseer

*Requirement: 2 advances in Manipulation (Leadership)*

You know how to direct others to perform tasks as effectively as you would yourself. As an Action, make a Challenging Manipulation (Leadership) test. On a success, your target may use your skill level instead of their own for their next test.

***

### Pathologist

*Requirement: 2 advances in Medicine (Autopsy)*

You have Advantage on tests to determine cause and manner of death, and to identify signs of disease, poison, or injury in a corpse.

***

### Phlebotomist

*Requirement: 3 advances in Medicine*

You are trained in bloodletting and can examine blood to diagnose illness. When you make a successful melee attack, you may choose to inflict the Bleeding condition instead of dealing damage.

***

### Porter

*Requirement: 2 advances in Athletics (Lifting)*

You can carry far more than most without slowing. Each Stamina point you invest in carrying counts as one category lighter for the purposes of encumbrance.

***

### Rapid Reload

*Requirements: 2 advances in each of Reflexes and Fighting (Archery or Thrown Weapons)*

You have drilled your reloading until it is as natural as breathing. This Trait may be taken multiple times. Each time, choose a ranged specialization; you may reload weapons of that type as a Free Action.

***

### Read Lips

*Requirements: 1 advance in each of Observation (Sight) and Academia (Linguistics)*

You have practised recognising speech through visual observation alone. Even if you cannot hear someone speak, you can understand their words by watching their lips and face.

***

### Scholar's Edge

*Requirement: 2 advances in Academia (Investigation or History)*

You are unusually adept at navigating documents, archives, and records. You have Advantage on tests to locate or retrieve information from written sources—scrolls, codices, wax tablets, or inscriptions. You read texts in half the normal time. If you take this Trait again, you also have Advantage on tests to determine when, where, how, or by whom a document was created.

***

### Secret Identity

You maintain a convincing cover within another social group or faction. Choose a faction and an appropriate role. As long as you are appropriately dressed and behaving in character, you gain +1 Influence with that faction. Maintaining the disguise may require Manipulation (Deception) tests when dealing with suspicious or perceptive members of that faction.

***

### Shock Assault

*Requirements: AGI 4, 3 advances in Stealth*

Those who strike first strike hardest. You may take 2 Actions on your turn during a Surprise Round.

***

### Skulker

*Requirements: 1 advance in each of Survival (Tracking) and Stealth*

You have a talent for following people without being noticed. When using Survival (Tracking) to shadow a target, you need not make Stealth tests to remain undetected—unless you do something to attract attention through carelessness or misfortune.

***

### Slippery

*Requirement: 2 advances in Reflexes*

You are extraordinarily difficult to pin down. When you break from engagement and an opponent makes an immediate attack against you, they have Disadvantage on that attack. If you take this Trait twice, you may Disengage as a Free Action.

***

### Spellcaster

*Requirement: Cannot have the Null Trait. This applies even at character creation.*

You have a gift for magic—whether through lineage, study, devotion, or accident. You may manifest spells and perform rituals (see Chapter 3). You may also purchase advances in the relevant casting skills and specializations.

When you gain this Trait, choose a casting tradition (Traditional, Research, or Dabbling) and gain one spell from that tradition's spellbook. You may spend 60 XP to learn additional minor spells, or 100 XP to learn spells of greater power.

***

### Sure-Footed

*Requirements: 1 advance in each of Athletics, Resilience (Fortitude), and Reflexes*

You maintain your footing where others stumble. If you have at least 1 point of Superiority, you become Fast. You have Advantage on tests to resist or end the Prone condition, may stand from Prone as a Free Action once per turn if not Restrained, and have Advantage on tests to resist being forcibly moved.

***

### Superior Commander

*Requirement: 4 advances in Manipulation (Leadership)*

You know how to translate a dominant position into decisive action. As an Action, choose a number of allies equal to your current Superiority. Each may immediately Move or take one Action. No character may benefit from this Trait more than once per round.

***

### Tactical Movement

*Requirements: 1 advance in each of Athletics and Resilience (Discipline)*

You move with calculated purpose, keeping cover between yourself and danger. You may Take Cover as a Free Action.

***

### Tenacious

You are hard to put down. Whenever you suffer the Stunned condition, you may immediately make a Challenging Resilience (Fortitude) test to shrug it off.

***

### Two-Handed Cleave

*Requirement: 2 advances in Fighting (Two-handed Weapons)*

You have practiced wide, devastating strokes with large weapons intended to carve through ranks of enemies. When you wield a two-handed melee weapon, it gains the Spread trait, and attacks you make with it cannot strike you.

This Trait may be taken multiple times. Each time after the first, choose one:

- **Brutalist:** The weapon also gains the Rend (+1) trait.
- **Leg Sweep:** When you hit a target in the leg, you may halve the damage to force them to make a Hard Reflexes (Balance) test or fall Prone.

***

### Unflinching Presence

*Requirement: 3 advances in Manipulation (Leadership)*

Your composure in the face of danger steadies the hearts of those around you. Whenever your side would lose Superiority, make a Hard Manipulation (Leadership) test. On a success, Superiority is not lost.

***

### Unremarkable

*Requirements: No advances in Manipulation or any Manipulation specialization, unless gained through character creation*

You attract no attention, project no status, and are easily forgotten. You are simply another face. When dressed appropriately, you may blend into most environments without a test—a monk in a scriptorium, a servant at a feast, a soldier in a camp. People of importance will generally ignore you entirely unless attention is drawn to you. This Trait allows you to move unnoticed and eavesdrop freely, but imposes Disadvantage on Manipulation and Rapport tests targeting high-status individuals. Additionally, the group does not lose Superiority when you suffer a Critical Wound or die.

***

### Well-Prepared

You are in the habit of anticipating what a difficult situation might require. Once per session, you may produce a small piece of equipment from your kit that you could plausibly have acquired before the session began—a length of rope, a particular herbal compound, a wax seal, a small tool. The item must be Encumbrance 0 or equivalent. When you use this Trait, deduct its cost from your coin, representing expenditure made before play.

***
